Heavy Air

Owen Hopper

Visuals by Eric Sheffield

Any number of musicians and instruments can accompany or augment the two laptop computer parts. The two laptop parts may and should be doubled to your desired ensemble. Spatialization may be appropriate and necessary, by having members of the ensemble seated around and throughout the audience with their sound emanating from their location.

The entire piece is built around a handful of sonorities drawn from recordings of resonant metal found objects. The goal is to fuse the spectral ‘signature’ of these original materials with an exploration and abstraction of them as ‘sonic objects’. The length of a performance should last as long as it takes for the ensemble to realize this goal.

Clix

Ge Wang

Visuals by Eric Sheffield

In this piece, human operators type to make sounds, while their machines synthesize, synchronize, and spatialize the audio. Every key on the computer keyboard (upper/lower-case letters, numbers, symbols) is mapped to a distinct pitch (using the key's ASCII representation) and when pressed, emits a clicking sound that is synchronized in time to a common pulse. A (human) conductor coordinates frequency range, texture, movement, and timing.
